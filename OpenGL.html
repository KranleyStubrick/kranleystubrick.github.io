OpenGL 3D Rendering Project

Link to Repository

The project involves the utilization of OpenGL, an open-source graphics API, to develop a 3D rendered pyramid. The primary objective is to demonstrate proficiency in graphics programming and showcase the capabilities of OpenGL in rendering complex 3D graphics.

Key Components:

  1. Geometry Definition: Defining the geometric structure of a pyramid within a virtual 3D environment using OpenGL functions.
  2. Material Specification: Employing OpenGL to specify materials and textures, enhancing the visual appearance of the rendered pyramid.
  3. Transformation Operations: Implementing transformative operations to manipulate the pyramid's position, orientation, and scale within the 3D space.

Additionally, the project incorporates the Lorenz attractor, a mathematical model describing chaotic systems. This integration aims to showcase the application of OpenGL in dynamically visualizing complex mathematical patterns within the context of a 3D environment.

The presentation of this project within an online portfolio emphasizes the technical aspects of graphics programming, including geometry manipulation, material rendering, and the dynamic representation of mathematical models. Overall, the project serves as a practical illustration of the diverse applications of OpenGL in creating visually compelling and mathematically informed 3D graphics.
